Nekilnojamojo turto registras

Lithuania's Real Estate Register — the central public register of all immovable property rights, maintained by the State Enterprise Centre of Registers.

What Is Nekilnojamojo turto registras?

The Nekilnojamojo turto registras (Real Estate Register) is maintained by Registrų centras, a state enterprise under the Ministry of Justice. It is the authoritative record of ownership, mortgages, easements, and other rights affecting immovable property throughout Lithuania.

Registration is constitutive: ownership of Lithuanian property passes legally only upon entry in the register. A signed notarial deed without subsequent registration does not transfer ownership against third parties.

The register is integrated with the cadastre — each property has a unique cadastral number linking the physical description to the legal ownership record. The Registrų centras portal (regia.lt) provides online access to register information.

Before purchasing, a buyer should obtain a register certificate (registro pažyma) showing the current owner, any mortgages, and any restrictions. The certificate is available online within minutes and costs a small fee.

Lithuania is one of the most advanced EU member states for digital public services. The entire registration process — from notarial deed to completed entry — is conducted electronically, with average registration times of one to three working days.

Key Facts

  • Managed by Registrų centras (state enterprise)
  • Registration is constitutive — ownership passes on registration
  • Integrated with the cadastre via unique cadastral numbers
  • Online portal: regia.lt — searchable publicly
  • Average registration time: one to three working days

Common Mistake

Not checking the register before signing a preliminary agreement. Encumbrances including undisclosed mortgages and pre-emption rights can block or complicate the transaction.

Expert Tip

Order a full register extract (pažyma) for both the land parcel and the building separately — they can have different ownership entries, particularly for apartments in older co-ownership buildings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who submits the registration application?

The notary submits the application electronically immediately after the deed is signed. The buyer does not need to visit the register office.

Is there a stamp duty in Lithuania?

There is no stamp duty on property purchases. The main transaction costs are the notary fee and the registration fee, both of which are relatively modest.

Can foreigners own property in Lithuania?

EU citizens and companies can purchase freely. Non-EU nationals can purchase residential and commercial property but face restrictions on agricultural and forest land.
