Hipoteka

Hipoteka is the Lithuanian term for a mortgage — a real right registered in the Nekilnojamojo Turto Registras securing the lender's claim against real property in the event of borrower default.

In Lithuania, a hipoteka is established by a notarially certified mortgage agreement and registered in the NTR encumbrances section. Once registered, the hipoteka is effective as a real right against any subsequent owner of the property — it follows the property regardless of ownership changes.

Lithuanian mortgage law is governed by the Civil Code (Civilinis kodeksas) and the Law on Mortgage. Banks offer fixed and variable rate hipoteka products; variable rate loans are typically linked to EURIBOR. The Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas) regulates mortgage lending and sets LTV limits.

When purchasing Lithuanian property subject to an existing hipoteka, the purchase contract should provide for simultaneous discharge of the mortgage at closing. The notary coordinates loan repayment and the išregistravimas (deregistration) of the hipoteka from the NTR.

The Bank of Lithuania caps LTV ratios for residential mortgages at 85% for primary residence purchases. Borrowers with existing mortgage commitments face lower effective LTV limits under stress-testing rules. Down payments of at least 15% are required.

Common Mistake: Completing a Lithuanian property purchase without confirming that the seller's hipoteka will be deregistered from the NTR at closing. A registered hipoteka is a real right that follows the property and binds the new owner unless formally removed.
Expert Tip: Include a clause in the pirkimo-pardavimo sutartis requiring the notary to withhold the sale proceeds from the seller until confirmed išregistravimas of any existing hipoteka from the NTR. Lithuanian notaries are experienced in coordinating this.

After repaying a Lithuanian hipoteka, request the lender's sutikimas išregistruoti (consent to deregistration) and submit an application to the NTR through the Registrų centras. The hipoteka is typically removed within one to three working days.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is išregistravimas in Lithuanian mortgage law?

Išregistravimas is the deregistration or removal of a hipoteka from the NTR encumbrances section. It requires the lender's sutikimas išregistruoti (consent to deregister) and an application to the Registrų centras.

What is the minimum deposit required for a Lithuanian residential mortgage?

The Bank of Lithuania requires a minimum 15% deposit for residential mortgage purchases, implying a maximum LTV of 85%. Borrowers with existing mortgage commitments may face lower effective LTV limits under additional stress-testing rules.

How long does hipoteka deregistration take in Lithuania?

Following submission of the lender's sutikimas išregistruoti to the Registrų centras, the hipoteka entry is typically removed from the NTR within one to three working days. The digital system allows fast processing.
