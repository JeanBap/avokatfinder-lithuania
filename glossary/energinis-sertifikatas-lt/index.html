Energinis Naudingumo Sertifikatas

The energinis naudingumo sertifikatas is Lithuania's mandatory energy performance certificate that classifies a building's energy efficiency from A++ to G and must be provided to buyers or tenants before any property sale or rental is concluded.

Lithuania transposed the EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive through the Law on Construction and associated technical regulations, making the energinis naudingumo sertifikatas compulsory for all buildings sold or rented. The certificate assesses the building's annual primary energy consumption per square metre and assigns a class from A++ (most efficient) to G (least efficient).

Certificates are issued by accredited energy specialists (atestuoti energetikos ekspertai) following a site assessment. The assessment covers the building envelope (insulation, windows, roof), heating, cooling, ventilation, hot water systems, and in commercial buildings, lighting. The certificate is valid for 10 years and must accompany property advertisements.

Lithuania's apartment building stock, largely built between 1960 and 1990 using Soviet-era large-panel construction (blokiniai namai), typically falls in the E to G range. The state renovation programme targets these buildings specifically, aiming to bring them to at least class C, and preferably B or A, post-renovation.

A buyer comparing apartments in similarly priced buildings should use the energy class as a proxy for future heating costs. A G-rated uninsulated Soviet block can consume four to six times more heating energy per square metre than a renovated B-class equivalent. In Lithuania's cold winters, this difference translates to hundreds of euros per year in utility bills.

Sellers who fail to provide a valid energy certificate may face administrative sanctions. The certificate must be registered in the national Information System of Permits for Construction (PLSIS) and in the Registrų centras. Buyers can verify certificate status online before purchase.

A G-class building in the programme queue may become B-class within a few years, substantially improving both comfort and resale value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a certificate needed for a house extension?

A new or substantially extended building requires a fresh energy certificate. A minor extension that does not materially affect the building's energy performance may not trigger a new certificate requirement.

Where can I verify a building's energy certificate?

Certificates are registered in the PLSIS (State Territorial Planning and Construction Inspectorate system). Your notary or lawyer can retrieve the certificate for any registered building.

What class must new buildings achieve?

Buildings with planning permission granted after 2021 must meet the Nearly Zero Energy Building standard, corresponding to class A or A++ in Lithuanian notation.
