Turto Vertinimas

Turto vertinimas is the professional valuation of property in Lithuania carried out by a certified property valuer (turto vertintojas) to determine market value for sale, mortgage, tax, insurance, or legal purposes.

A turto vertinimas (property valuation or appraisal) in Lithuania is a formal written assessment of a property's market or other defined value, prepared by a certified property valuer (sertifikuotas turto vertintojas) under the Law on Valuation of Property and Business. Banks require an independent valuation before approving a mortgage; courts order valuations in succession, compulsory purchase, and dispute proceedings.

Property valuers in Lithuania must hold a certificate issued by the Lithuanian Association of Property Valuers (Lietuvos turto vertintojų asociacija, LTVA) or another accredited body. The valuation profession is regulated by the Law on Valuation and applies European Valuation Standards (EVS) aligned with RICS and IVSC frameworks.

The primary method for residential property valuation is the comparative approach (lyginamoji vertinimo metoda), in which the valuer analyses recent transactions of comparable properties from the Registrų centras transaction database. The income approach is used for investment properties; the cost approach for unique or special-purpose buildings.

A bank valuation (banko užsakyta vertinimo ataskaita) is commissioned by the lender and its primary purpose is to protect the bank's security. It may be more conservative than an open-market estimate. Buyers should understand that if the bank's valuation is below the agreed purchase price, the loan-to-value ratio will be calculated on the lower figure, requiring a larger equity contribution.

Lithuania's Registrų centras publishes aggregated transaction data publicly, enabling buyers and agents to research market prices independently. The database, accessible via regia.lt, shows average price per square metre by property type, age band, and municipality — valuable context for assessing whether an asking price is reasonable before engaging a valuer.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes treat an estate agent's price opinion as equivalent to a certified valuation. An agent's market appraisal is not a statutory valuation and is not accepted by banks or courts. Always ensure any valuation used for mortgage or legal purposes is from a certified LTVA-registered valuer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a valuation legally required before selling property in Lithuania?

Not for private sales. It is required for mortgage lending, certain tax purposes, estate administration, and compulsory purchase proceedings.

How current must a valuation be for mortgage purposes?

Banks typically require a valuation not older than 6–12 months, with more recent valuations preferred in volatile markets.

Can I contest the bank's valuation if I think it is too low?

Yes, you can commission an independent valuation from a different certified valuer and present it to the bank. Banks may accept a second opinion or seek a review. There is no guarantee of a different outcome, but the option exists.
