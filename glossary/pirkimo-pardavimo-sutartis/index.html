Legal Term

What is Pirkimo-pardavimo sutartis?

A Lithuanian purchase and sale agreement — the notarially authenticated contract that transfers ownership of immovable property and must be registered in the Real Estate Register.

Key Facts — Pirkimo-pardavimo sutartis
  • Must be notarially authenticated — unnotarised agreements cannot transfer title
  • Notary fees regulated and capped by ministerial order
  • Preliminary agreement (preliminari sutartis) should also be notarised
  • Deposit typically 10% of purchase price
  • Registration follows automatically via notary's electronic submission

A pirkimo-pardavimo sutartis (purchase and sale agreement) for immovable property in Lithuania must be executed before a notary (notaras). Both parties sign in the notary's presence after the notary has verified their identity, explained the legal consequences, and read the deed aloud.

The agreement records the price, a description of the property by cadastral reference, warranties about title and condition, and the parties' instructions to the notary to submit the registration application.

Lithuanian notaries are state-licensed legal professionals with a monopoly on authenticating property transactions. Their fees are regulated by the Minister of Justice and scale with the transaction value. The notary is neutral and does not act as an advocate for either party.

A preliminary agreement (preliminari sutartis) is commonly used to secure a transaction. It must be in writing and notarised to be fully enforceable. The deposit paid under the preliminary agreement (avansas or užstatas) is typically 10% of the price.

Following notarisation, the notary submits the registration application to the Nekilnojamojo turto registras electronically. Registration is normally completed within one to three working days, at which point the buyer becomes the legal owner.

Common Mistake: Relying on a simple written preliminary agreement without notarisation. While binding in contract law, an unnotarised preliminary agreement may not be enforceable by specific performance if the seller refuses to complete.
Expert Tip: Negotiate a penalty clause (netesybos) in the preliminary agreement — typically double the deposit forfeited by the defaulting party. This is standard practice in Lithuania and deters either side from pulling out.

Frequently Asked Questions

What taxes does a buyer pay?

There is no stamp duty. Real estate acquisition tax (nekilnojamojo turto mokestis) is payable annually by the owner, not on purchase. The buyer pays the notary fee and registration fee.

Can I sign with a power of attorney?

Yes. A notarised power of attorney (įgaliojimas) allows a representative to sign on your behalf. Remote signing via video notarisation is also available.

What if the property has a mortgage?

The seller must discharge the mortgage at or before completion. The notary will check the register before the deed signing and will not proceed if there is an unresolved mortgage without a release undertaking.
