Daugiabučio Namo Bendrija

A daugiabučio namo bendrija is the apartment building community association in Lithuania, the legal entity of apartment owners that manages common areas, collects maintenance contributions, and organises renovation of multi-unit residential buildings.

Lithuanian law (Law on the Maintenance and Renovation of Apartment Buildings) requires that owners in multi-unit residential buildings (daugiabučiai namai) organise the management of common areas through one of three models: a bendrija (community association), a jungtinės veiklos sutartis (joint activity agreement), or a professional administratorius appointed by the municipality. The bendrija is the most common form in owner-managed buildings.

The bendrija is a legal entity registered with the Juridinių asmenų registras (Register of Legal Entities). It has a board (valdyba) elected by the general meeting of owners, a manager (administratorius, either elected or hired), and its own bank account. All apartment owners are members automatically by their ownership of a unit.

Monthly maintenance contributions (mokesčiai už bendrojo naudojimo objektų priežiūrą) are set by the general meeting and cover communal electricity, cleaning, lift maintenance, minor repairs, and building insurance. Separate contributions to the atnaujinimo fondas (renovation fund) accumulate capital for major works.

Since 2021 Lithuania has pursued an ambitious multi-apartment building renovation programme (daugiabučių namų atnaujinimo programa) financed substantially by EU structural funds and state subsidies. Buildings achieving NZEB standards post-renovation receive the highest subsidy rates. Many buildings that joined the programme have achieved dramatic energy rating improvements.

Buyers of apartments should request the bendrija's latest annual accounts, the current renovation fund balance, and any renovation programme commitments. Buildings enrolled in the state programme will have compulsory repayment obligations attached to the apartments — typically a monthly loan repayment for 20 years. This obligation transfers to new owners.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I opt out of the bendrija?

No. Membership is automatic. If the building has no bendrija, the municipality appoints an administrator — owners cannot escape the management framework.

What if the bendrija is poorly managed?

Owners can call a general meeting to replace the board or engage a professional management company. Persistent mismanagement can be reported to the municipality.

Does the renovation loan transfer to the new owner on sale?

Yes. The renovation loan is attached to the property, not the individual. It transfers with the apartment. The outstanding balance must be disclosed in the purchase agreement.
