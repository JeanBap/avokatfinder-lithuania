Registro Išrašas

A registro išrašas is an official extract from Lithuania's Real Property Register (Nekilnojamojo turto registras) that confirms the property's legal description, registered owner, and any encumbrances, mortgages, or restrictions affecting the title.

The Nekilnojamojo turto registras (Real Property Register) is maintained by the State Enterprise Centre of Registers (VĮ Registrų centras) and is the authoritative public record of all immovable property rights in Lithuania. A registro išrašas is a certified extract from this register that provides a snapshot of the property's legal status at the moment of issue.

The extract contains: the property's unique number (unikalus numeris), address, area, purpose, and cadastral data; the registered owner(s) and the basis of ownership (purchase, inheritance, gift, etc.); and a list of encumbrances — mortgages (hipotekos), easements (servitutai), leases registered for more than one year, court-ordered restrictions, and any temporary blocks on transfer.

An informative extract (informacinis išrašas) is available to anyone and can be obtained online via regia.lt for a small fee. It is sufficient for preliminary due diligence. The notary preparing the sale deed orders a certified extract (teisinis išrašas) directly from the register on the day of signing to capture the most current state of title.

Buyers should obtain a fresh informative extract before paying any deposit and should ask their notary to confirm there are no newly registered encumbrances immediately before signing the deed. Mortgages that have been repaid but not formally released remain visible in the register until a notarial release deed is filed.

Lithuania's register is highly digitised. Extracts are available in real time online, property history is searchable, and encumbrances are typically registered within minutes of a notarial filing. This transparency significantly reduces the risk of title fraud compared to less digitised systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a registro israsas valid?

There is no formal expiry for the informative extract. However, the register is updated in real time, so any extract becomes stale the moment a new encumbrance is registered. Refresh it immediately before the signing appointment.

Can I see historical ownership records?

Yes. The register records the full ownership history with dates and basis of transfer. Historical encumbrances that have been released are also visible with their cancellation dates.

What does a temporary block on transfer mean?

A temporary block (laikinas apribojimas) may be placed by a court, a creditor, or in connection with a notarial procedure in progress. The block prevents transfer until the underlying cause is resolved. Your lawyer should investigate immediately.
