Statybos Leidimas

Statybos leidimas is the building permit in Lithuania issued by the State Territorial Planning and Construction Inspectorate (VTPSI) that authorises construction, major extension, or significant renovation of a building before any qualifying works may commence.

Under the Lithuanian Law on Construction (Statybos įstatymas), a statybos leidimas is required for the construction of new buildings, substantial extensions, structural alterations, change of use, and major renovation works. The permit is applied for online through the PLSIS (Permit and Construction Information System) portal and is issued by the VTPSI.

The application requires a construction project (statybos projektas) prepared by a licensed designer (projektavimo licencija), a land plot ownership document, confirmation that the project conforms to the local zoning plan (teritorijų planavimo dokumentas), and certain specialist assessments (archaeological, environmental, heritage) where applicable.

Once granted, the statybos leidimas is valid for three years within which construction must begin. The permit may be extended by a further three years upon application. Permits lapse if construction does not begin within the validity period and a new application is required.

During construction, the works must be supervised by a technical supervisor (statinio techninės priežiūros vadovas) and a design supervisor (statinio projekto vykdymo priežiūros vadovas). Both must be licensed. Their declarations of conformity are required to obtain the completion certificate.

Buyers of new-build properties and land for development should verify the permit status in the PLSIS portal. Permits, associated project documentation, and completion certificates are publicly accessible. A property marketed without a valid permit is a significant legal risk — construction without a permit is illegal and subject to demolition orders.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers of rural plots sometimes trust a seller's assurance that a building permit is 'straightforward to obtain'. Zoning restrictions, protected area overlays, and utilities access requirements frequently make rural development far more complex and costly than expected. Always obtain the permit before purchasing land for development.

This free document tells you exactly what class of building, height, and footprint ratio is permitted on the specific plot — eliminating guesswork about development potential.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a building permit required for internal renovation?

Generally no — internal works that do not affect load-bearing structure, facade, or engineering systems do not require a permit. A simplified construction notification (statybos pranešimas) may suffice for intermediate works.

Can I see the building permit for a property I am buying?

Yes. Permits are public records in the PLSIS system. Search by the property's address or unique number (unikalus numeris) to see permit history and status.

What if the building was constructed without a permit?

Illegal construction (savavališka statyba) is subject to enforcement by VTPSI. Options range from legalisation (if technically possible) to mandatory demolition. Buyers of properties with unpermitted structures carry this risk.
