PVM Nekilnojamajam Turtui

PVM nekilnojamajam turtui refers to Lithuanian Value Added Tax (Pridėtinės vertės mokestis) as it applies to property transactions, particularly first sales of new buildings by VAT-registered developers, which are subject to 21% VAT unless a reduced rate or exemption applies.

In Lithuania, VAT (PVM — Pridėtinės vertės mokestis) applies to property transactions when the seller is a VAT-registered entity conducting a business activity — typically a developer constructing and selling new residential or commercial buildings. Private individuals selling their personal homes are not subject to PVM on the sale price.

The standard PVM rate in Lithuania is 21%. However, a reduced rate of 9% applies to the supply of housing — specifically the construction and first sale of residential apartments and houses — under specific conditions set by the VAT Law and Government resolutions. The 9% rate has been periodically revised, so buyers and developers must confirm the current rate and qualifying conditions with a tax adviser.

The second or subsequent sale of a residential building more than two years after first occupation is generally exempt from PVM. This means that resales of existing apartments between private sellers do not attract PVM. Buyers purchasing from a developer for the first time should confirm whether PVM is included in or added to the stated price.

Commercial property transactions between VAT-registered entities are often subject to a PVM reversal charge mechanism or may be structured to allow VAT recovery by the buyer. The appropriate treatment depends on the parties' VAT status and the intended use of the property. Commercial property acquisitions should always be reviewed by a Lithuanian tax adviser.

When PVM applies, it must be clearly stated in the contract and the notarial deed. The notary will ask parties to confirm the VAT treatment before authenticating the deed. Misclassification of PVM liability can result in tax authority assessments and penalties for both buyer and seller.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers of new-build apartments sometimes assume the advertised price is VAT-inclusive. Developers sometimes quote prices net of PVM and add it at the notarial stage. Always ask in writing whether the agreed price includes PVM before signing any preliminary agreement.
💡 Expert TipFor a new-build purchase, request a written confirmation from the developer stating the applicable PVM rate and whether the quoted price is inclusive or exclusive of tax. Keep this document alongside the purchase agreement in case of future disputes or tax authority inquiries.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does PVM apply to the purchase of land?

The sale of building land (land with planning permission for construction) by a developer is generally subject to PVM. Agricultural land and land without building permission may be exempt. Classify carefully with a tax adviser.

Can I recover PVM if I am a VAT-registered company buying for business use?

If the property will be used for PVM-taxable business activities, input PVM may generally be deducted from output PVM liabilities. Complex rules apply; professional tax advice is essential.

Is PVM payable on notary fees?

Yes. Notarial services are subject to 21% PVM, charged in addition to the regulated notarial tariff.
