Žemės Mokestis

Žemės mokestis is Lithuania's annual land tax levied on land owners or users, calculated as a percentage of the land's taxable value and payable to the municipality in which the land is located.

Lithuania's žemės mokestis (land tax) is an annual tax on the ownership or use of land, regulated by the Law on Land Tax. The tax applies to all categories of land — residential, agricultural, commercial, and forest — with different rates and exemptions depending on the land's designated purpose and the owner's circumstances.

The tax base is the taxable value (mokestinė vertė) of the land plot, determined by the State Enterprise Centre of Registers (Registrų centras) through mass valuation exercises. The taxable value is updated periodically and is publicly searchable through the Registrų centras portal (regia.lt). It may differ significantly from market value.

Municipal councils set the annual tax rate within bounds set by national legislation, typically between 0.01% and 4% of taxable value per annum. Different rates may apply to different land categories within the same municipality. Agricultural land and forestry land generally attract lower rates than urban development land.

Certain categories are exempt or reduced: land under owner-occupied residential buildings may benefit from a reduced rate; agricultural land cultivated by the owner may qualify for exemptions; and low-income pensioners may receive relief under municipal discretion. Buyers should confirm the applicable rate and any exemptions for their specific plot.

Unpaid žemės mokestis creates a tax debt enforceable by the State Tax Inspectorate (Valstybinė mokesčių inspekcija, VMI). Tax debts can attract interest and ultimately result in a lien on the land. Buyers should request a tax compliance certificate (pažyma apie mokesčių įsiskolinimą) before purchase.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers of agricultural land sometimes assume žemės mokestis is negligible. For large plots with high taxable values or commercial classifications, the annual liability can be substantial. Always check regia.lt for the taxable value before purchase.
💡 Expert TipVerify the land's taxable value and current municipality rate before making an offer. Also confirm that the land classification in the Registrų centras matches the use you intend — mismatches can result in back-tax assessments when reclassification is triggered by a permit application.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does zemes mokestis apply to buildings?

No. Žemės mokestis is levied only on land. Buildings and structures are subject to a separate property tax (nekilnojamojo turto mokestis) for legal entities; private residential buildings owned by individuals are generally exempt from that tax.

How often is the taxable value updated?

Mass valuations are conducted by Registrų centras periodically — typically every few years. The most recent valuation year applies until a new exercise is completed.

Who pays if the land is leased to a third party?

Under Lithuanian law, the land user (tenant under a long-term lease) may be liable for žemės mokestis if the lease agreement transfers that obligation. Check the lease terms carefully.
