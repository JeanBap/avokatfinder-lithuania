Atnaujinimo Fondas

The atnaujinimo fondas is the building renovation reserve fund in Lithuania, maintained by apartment building community associations to accumulate capital for major maintenance and renovation works on multi-unit residential buildings.

Lithuanian legislation requires apartment building associations (bendrijai) and administrators to collect monthly contributions into a dedicated atnaujinimo fondas (renovation fund). The fund is held in a separate bank account and may be used only for major capital works — roof replacement, facade renovation, window upgrades, heating system modernisation, lift overhaul, and similar significant expenditure.

The minimum monthly contribution rate is prescribed by municipal decisions or agreed at the general meeting, expressed in euros per square metre of apartment area. Contributions vary widely between buildings depending on age, condition, and planned works. Buildings undergoing state-supported renovation commit to higher contribution rates as part of the programme conditions.

Unlike routine maintenance fees, the atnaujinimo fondas cannot be used for cleaning, minor repairs, or administration. Withdrawals require a decision of the general meeting specifying the works. The board presents annual accounts to the general meeting, with the fund balance as a key item.

A low atnaujinimo fondas balance in an ageing building with known maintenance needs is a significant risk for buyers. When the fund is insufficient for urgent works, the association must levy a special assessment (papildomas įnašas) on all owners, or apply to the state renovation programme for subsidised financing.

The state renovation programme for multi-apartment buildings (administered by the Housing Energy Efficiency Agency, BETA) provides subsidised loans and grants for comprehensive renovations. Buildings that qualify receive capital works financed largely upfront, with owners repaying a portion over 20 years via small monthly amounts attached to the apartment.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers look at the monthly maintenance fee and renovation fund contribution but not the fund balance. A building with five years of contributions but a planned facade renovation costing €200,000 may have a €50,000 shortfall — meaning a large special levy after completion.
💡 Expert TipAsk the bendrija administrator for the current atnaujinimo fondas balance and the building's 5-year capital expenditure plan. Compare the balance against planned works. If the building is enrolled in the BETA programme, obtain the loan agreement to understand the monthly repayment obligation that will transfer to you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the renovation fund be invested to earn interest?

Funds can be held in interest-bearing accounts but Lithuanian law restricts their use. Any interest earned is credited to the fund balance.

What if I sell before a major renovation is completed?

The fund balance stays with the building. You cannot withdraw your proportional share. The new owner inherits both the fund balance and any renovation commitments or loan repayments.

Is the renovation fund separate from the EU renovation loan?

Yes. The renovation fund is the association's own savings. The EU-subsidised BETA loan is external financing, repaid via separate monthly instalments attached to each apartment.
