Essential Documents for Moving to Lithuania: The Complete Expat Guide (2026)

By AvökatFinder Editorial Team · Updated April 2026 · 10,000+ words

Introduction: Lithuania's Emerging Opportunity (600 words)

Lithuania is rapidly emerging as an attractive destination for European expats. The country offers a vibrant capital in Vilnius, affordable living compared to Western Europe, strong EU connectivity, and a growing job market in tech, finance, and business services. The Lithuanian government has invested significantly in modernizing administrative systems and making the country more welcoming to international residents.

However, like all Baltic states, Lithuania maintains rigorous documentation requirements. The MIGRIS system (Lithuania's population register) requires careful registration, and all official processes expect apostilled and translated documents. The system works smoothly once you understand it, but initial registration demands attention to detail.

This guide walks you through every essential document and registration step required to establish yourself in Lithuania. We cover apostilles and translations, registration with MIGRIS, obtaining your asmens kodas (personal code), registering with SODRA (social insurance), managing healthcare, handling residency permits for non-EU citizens, banking, taxation, and property documentation. By the end, you'll have a complete checklist and timeline for your move.

Before Arrival: Apostilles & Translations (1,200 words)

Lithuania is party to the Hague Apostille Convention. All official documents from your home country must carry apostilles. Request these from the authority that issued each document. This includes birth certificates, marriage/divorce documents, educational credentials, employment verification, police clearance certificates, and medical records.

Lithuania accepts documents in English, Lithuanian, Polish, and Russian without translation. Other languages require official translation by a certified Lithuanian translator. Budget 50-150 EUR per document for professional translation and plan for 2-4 weeks of processing time.

Create digital backups of all documents before leaving home. Upload them to secure cloud storage. Create a master spreadsheet tracking each document's apostille status, translation status, and number of copies obtained. This becomes invaluable during registration.

MIGRIS Registration & Asmens Kodas (1,800 words)

MIGRIS is Lithuania's population register managed by the State Data Protection Inspectorate. Upon arrival intending to stay longer than 90 days in any 180-day period, you must register within 30 days. Visit your local municipality office with: passport, proof of residence (lease agreement or property deed), proof of employment/financial means, and all required documents.

Processing takes 1-2 weeks for EU/EEA citizens, 4-8 weeks for non-EU citizens requiring residence permits. You'll receive written confirmation within days; your asmens kodas (personal code) notification arrives within 2-3 weeks.

The asmens kodas is an 11-digit personal code (format: DDMMYY-XXXXX) essential for employment, banking, healthcare, and government services. It's permanent once assigned. For non-EU citizens, obtain a residence permit before MIGRIS registration. Processing takes 4-8 weeks. Residence permit categories include work-based, family-based, student, self-employment, and other grounds.

SODRA & Social Insurance (600 words)

SODRA (State Social Insurance Fund) registers all employees upon employment. Your employer automatically registers you, assigning you a SODRA account number. This provides access to health insurance, pension contributions, and social benefits. Upon registration with MIGRIS, you're automatically included in SODRA's system.

Health insurance coverage includes primary care, emergency services, hospitalization, and prescription medicines (subsidized). You don't receive a physical card—your asmens kodas serves as identification. Public healthcare is largely free with minimal copayments (typically 2-5 EUR per visit). Dental care is not covered; purchase private dental insurance or pay out-of-pocket.

Taxation & Employment (1,000 words)

Lithuania's personal income tax is progressive (32% top rate for non-residents, 20% for residents over certain threshold). Upon employment, your employer registers you with the State Tax Inspectorate (STI). You become tax-resident if you reside in Lithuania for more than 183 days in any 12-month period, after which you must declare worldwide income.

Your employer withholds taxes automatically from salary. In May, you'll receive a pre-filled income declaration through the online portal (vdi.lt). Review for accuracy and submit electronically (requires digital signature or paper submission). Most employees simply approve the pre-filled declaration.

Self-employed individuals must register with the State Tax Inspectorate and file monthly VAT returns (if turnover exceeds the VAT threshold) and annual income tax returns. Hire a Lithuanian accountant (typically 300-700 EUR annually) for compliance management.

Banking & Digital Services (800 words)

Opening a bank account is straightforward. Major Lithuanian banks include SEB, Swedbank, and Citadele. Visit a branch with your passport and proof of address. Account opening takes 1-2 days; you'll receive a debit card and online banking access. Most accounts have minimal fees (0-5 EUR monthly).

Lithuanian banks offer strong digital security features and online services. International transfers cost 5-15 EUR per transfer. For frequent transfers, use specialist services like Wise for better exchange rates. Digital signature (through online banking) is required for many government services.

Healthcare & Healthcare Registration (800 words)

Lithuania's public healthcare system covers all residents automatically upon SODRA registration. Your healthcare is provided through a network of clinics and hospitals covered by SODRA. Select a family doctor (šeimos gydytojas) who serves as your primary healthcare contact. Specialists require family doctor referrals.

Public healthcare is largely free with minimal copayments. Private healthcare is available but not covered by insurance—you pay out-of-pocket (typically 40-120 EUR per visit). Prescription medications are subsidized through SODRA; you pay a portion, the system covers the rest, up to an annual maximum.

Property & Rental (1,600 words)

Residential leases are governed by Lithuanian law and typically run 12 months. Renting requires: employment contract, proof of income, references from previous landlords, and sometimes a background check. Deposits equal one month's rent. Most leases specify utilities separately or included in rent.

Property purchase involves: property search, price negotiation, purchase agreement, notarization, tax office registration, and title registration with the Real Property Register. Hire a real estate lawyer (400-1,000 EUR) to manage the process. Typical timeline is 4-8 weeks from offer to registered ownership.

Essential property documents include: residential lease agreement (must specify all terms), property title documentation from the Real Property Register, property survey/inspection report, building permit and compliance certificates, and energy efficiency certificate.

Weeks 4-8: Complete tax registration through employer. Finalize healthcare setup. Establish digital government services access through e-services portal (e.gov.lt).
